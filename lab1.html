Casablanca - 1941

Widely considered one of the greatest movies of all time, Casablanca definitely lives up to this reputation. Set in the namesake city in French Morocco during the early years of World War II, this movie follows American Rick Blaine (Humphrey Bogart) as he struggles to run his popular nightclub amid pressure from the French police, under command of the Nazi forces. Rick's life is further complicated when his former lover, Ilsa (Ingrid Bergman), appears with her husband, Victor Laszlo (Paul Henreid), who is an Allied collaborator wanted by the Nazis. Rick must decide whether to help Ilsa and Laszlo escape Casablanca, or turn Laszlo in and rekindle his relationship with Ilsa.

On the Waterfront - 1954

Marlon Brando stars as Terry Malloy in this story of fear and oppression on the Hoboken waterfront. Employed as a goon for local union boss Johnny Friendly, Terry unwittingly gets wrapped up in Friendly's scheme to take out anyone who opposes his rule and attempts to rat him out to the Waterfront Commission. Once loyal to Friendly, Terry is conflicted when he encounters Friendly's enemy, Catholic priest Father Barry (Karl Malden), who is dead set on ending the corruption and abuse occuring to the dockworkers. Terry also begins to fall in love with sister of a friend killed by Johnny Friendly, Evie Doyle (Eva Marie Saint), further complicating his standings. Terry must now decide what is right, and what he is willing to give up to do what is right.

The Graduate - 1967

Mike Nichols' generational classic, The Graduate is every bit as entertaining today as it was back in 1967. The movie follows the story of Benjamin Braddock, played by Dustin Hoffman. Benjamin is the embodiment of every young adult of the 1960s and even today: confused. On his return to Los Angeles from going to college in the east, Benjamin realizes that he has no idea what to do with his life. Feeling that no one understands him, he is thrown into an affair with the much older wife of his father's business partner, Mrs. Robinson (Anne Bancroft). As Benjamin attempts to figure out where he is meant to be, his forced date with the Robinson's daughter, Elaine (Katharine Ross), teaches him that he is not alone in his struggle.
